Top Military Museums in the United States

National Museum of the Marine Corps

The National Museum of the Marine Corps opened in 2006 as a monument to 'Honor, Courage & Commitment' of our nation's Marines. The museum features many armed conflicts in which many Marines served in, dating all the way back to the creation of the Marine Corps during the American Revoltion. They are currently expanding the museum to include new exhibits that will open in phases through the year 2022.

The National WWII Museum

The National WWII Museum, formerly known as The National D-Day Museum, is a military history museum located in New Orleans, Louisiana. It was opened on the 56th anniversary of D-Day as a tribute and rememberance to all those who served in WWII. One exciting feature of this museum is the opportunity to ride a PT-305; an armed naval vessile used by the Americans in a multitude of sea operations in the later years of the war.

National Museum of the United States Air Force

The National Museum of the United States Air Force is the official museum of the United States Air Force located at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base. The NMUSAF is the oldest and largest military aviation museum in the world, with more than 360 aircraft and missiles on display.

USS Midway Museum

The USS Midway Museum is a maritime museum located in downtown San Diego at Navy Pier. The ship houses an extensive collection of aircraft, many of which were built in Southern California. Upon boarding the museum, you will be able to visit nearly all levels of the ship, ranging from the flight deck to the captain's quarters. See how a aircraft carrier operats and its crew lives all at the USS Midway Museum.
